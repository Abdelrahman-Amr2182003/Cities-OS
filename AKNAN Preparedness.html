Topic 1

A metropolitan population’s partial or complete horizontal evacuation is an important protective precaution against a natural or technological hazard. As a result of noncompliance with instructions, accidents, traffic jams, incivilities, a lack of preparation for civil security, or increased exposure to dangers, casualties may be significant during enormous displacement in the context of stress and a potentially deteriorated environment.

Topic 2

Working on evacuation plans is crucial for preventing casualties caused by improvisation and encouraging self-evacuation whenever possible. Due to the impossibility of recreating the conditions of a crisis on the ground to evaluate such evacuation plans, it is necessary to construct realistic simulation models to evaluate them. Several patents explored and investigated simulation models to implement evacuation based on ‘realistic’ conditions.

Topic 3

Not just the cases of disaster that require optimizing evacuation routes but more frequently are the cases of new city planning and temporary closures for maintenance, renovations, or city-scale events that easily affect the pedestrian and vehicle flow. Several studies and IPs on natural disasters and emergencies have investigated subjects, including optimizing evacuation route selection, creating evacuation maps, and building disaster communication systems. Generally, a multi-agent simulation is essential in several disaster studies for modeling and assuming people’s evacuation behavior in crisis circumstances and in traffic clogging. AKNAN involves a set of key objectives: planning, taking data from sensors to fine-tune the model, and providing a predictive/management scheme for evacuation not just in disasters but also in a city-clogging event due to the mega event or maintenance use case.
