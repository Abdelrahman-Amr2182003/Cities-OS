Sensor-data integration and visualization

AKNAN creates an operational digital twin of the city, incorporating a flood resilience simulation module. By merging data from weather stations, river gauges, urban sensors, GIS tools, mapping tools, and satellite imagery, the digital twin provides high-fidelity 3D visualizations at a city scale.The Flood Digital Twin also assists maintenance crews by optimizing their routes during flood events. It integrates water level sensors and rainfall data, enabling real-time flood condition updates and helping crews locate manholes and evaluate the extent of flooding for efficient route planning.

Topic 2

With the Cities:OS digital twin solution, AKNAN, stakeholders can make data-driven decisions and strengthen the resilience of flood control infrastructure. By visualizing sensor placement and utilizing water height data, the solution enables optimization capabilities. It supports the gradual expansion of the sensor network, starting with a limited number and scaling up over time. This approach ensures long-term value and flexibility, allowing strategic placement of new sensors in critical areas as the network grows. Municipalities and city planners can make cost-effective investments and enhance overall flood monitoring capabilities by deploying sensors tailored to address specific flood-prone locations.

Simulation and scenario analysis

The natural disaster simulation environment in AKNAN, employs advanced algorithms and simulations to predict flood behavior, assess vulnerability, and evaluate the effectiveness of different flood mitigation measures. It visualizes the flooding scenarios from historical data of previous flooding scenarios or maintenance use cases to assess their impact on infrastructure services such as power lines, water and wastewater treatment networks, and roads. In terms of scenario simulations, Cities:OS allows users to simulate various flood scenarios, incorporating factors such as rainfall intensity, river flow rates, and hydrological characteristics. It comprehensively explains potential flood impacts on critical infrastructure, residential areas, and transportation networks.
