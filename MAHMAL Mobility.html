Topic 1

MAHMAL is a Cities:OS mobility use-case solution. Cities:OS provides the world’s only city-scale behavioral population model with scalable physics integration.

Topic 2

The MAHMAL application utilizes the interaction between this unique dynamic population model and the city’s transport infrastructure to provide AI-driven decision-making support tools to city planners and operators. These tools allow decision-makers to plan and operate city transport optimally modes to meet customer satisfaction.

Topic 3

Successful deployment of MaaS solutions can minimize emissions and even ameliorate other negative externalities such as traffic congestion, air pollution, social exclusion, and excessive space use. MaaS extends beyond a mere mobility app; it is a platform for providing multimodal mobility services, ideally leading to a more sustainable mobility system. Furthermore, MaaS products are generally open to mobility and may even include non-transportation-related services such as parking. In recent years, there has been a boom of different mobility technologies that promise to deliver enough ease to persuade consumers to abandon their private automobiles in favor of other transportation modes included in their MaaS bundles. Many of the current MaaS studies have dived into the demand side to discover prospective target groups and the desirability of certain MaaS bundle offerings to those groups.

Topic 5

Features
• Utilizing a probabilistic dynamic population model that captured the planning intervention-personal behavior between travelers and the built environment to identify the optimal locations for each mode of transportation. This includes public mode transportation wherein the pick-up and drop-off locations are fixed and the required for-hire vehicles wherein the pick-up and drop-off locations are flexible in a given location, either in pre-existing cities or for future planning of new smart cities.
• Identifying the detailed status of potential travelers including but not limited to their locations, travel preferences, level of income, the urgency of the trip, driving patterns, calendar information, traveler preferences, and interests, a medical or emotional condition of the customers, travel
• experience, preferred modes of transportation, frequent travel locations and other attributes assigned to the traveler as an asset. Enabling one or more travelers to issue an outgoing transfer request to transition from their original position to a destination position in a built environment that directly impact travel attributes, including but not limited to climate, traffic, building, legal constraints or needs, and transportation services.

Topic 6

• Managing transfer requests for trips that one or more travelers issue in a built environment within one or more regions such that it optimizes route selection for the trip(s), the total emissions of a said trip, the mode of transportation, trip priority, travel time among other personal, environmental and legal parameters to generate the travel bundles, which essentially comprises matching the selected mode of transportation, the selected route, and one or more travel suggestions, that best suits the travelers’ preferences as well as t among other governmental and environmental constraints to bring down the total sector emission to a certain target value.
• Fine-tuning said population model based on data collected from sensors across the city, historical travel data, public mode transportation reservation system, and travelers’ location profiles, and reservations, among other valuable travel data, are all employed utilized as an iterative feedback loop to train the model, increase its accuracy, and enhance the transfer request management block for a better-customized travel bundle.
